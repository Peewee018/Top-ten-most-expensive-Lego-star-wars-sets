The history

The partnership between LEGO and Star Wars has been a remarkable journey of collaboration, beginning in 1999 when the first LEGO Star Wars set was released. This alliance marked LEGO's first foray into licensing and set the stage for a new era of themed playsets. The early years focused on iconic vehicles and characters from the original Star Wars trilogy, capturing the imagination of fans across generations. As the Star Wars universe expanded with new films and animated series, so did the LEGO Star Wars line, introducing sets that depicted scenes and vehicles from the prequel trilogy and beyond. The acquisition of Lucasfilm by Disney in 2012 further solidified the partnership, allowing LEGO to produce sets based on the new films and series, including the highly popular Mandalorian series. This synergy has not only resulted in a diverse range of playsets and collectibles but also spawned video games and animated films, enriching the Star Wars narrative in the LEGO context. The collaboration has been celebrated with special edition sets and continues to evolve, with both entities looking forward to more innovative creations that will delight fans for years to come. The enduring appeal of LEGO Star Wars is a testament to the timeless allure of both brands and their ability to inspire creativity and storytelling.
